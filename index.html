My name is Markus Müller

I have a M.Sc. in Business Psychology and I am currently studying Applied Computer Science (B.Sc.) in the 2nd semester.

During my years at the university and internships, I always had the most fun when it came to evaluating data and visualize it in presentations.

Already during my studies I liked to work with SPSS (software for statistical data analysis) and was always responsible for the data analysis in every group task. In my bachelor thesis, I used a two-factor analysis of Covariance with two covariates and in my master thesis, I used a Moderated Multiple Regression to answer my research questions.

After my Master's in Business Psychology, I started learning Python out of the interest in statistical analysis of data. I started with the standard libraries for data analysis: NumPy (for mathematical, statistical calculations), Pandas (representation of data in a DataFrame (comparable to Excel) and Matplotlib for visualization of data. I learnd from Udemy-courses, youtube videos and free books on that topic. These Udemy courses also included Machine Learning. So I got an introduction to Machine Learning with the scitkit-learn library (the standard library for machine learning). To get more serious I succefully completed the nanodegrees for Data Analyst and Data Scientist from Udacity. These courses where more advanced and had a strong focus on data gathering and data wrangling and preprocessing, which was somewhat new to me, since I was working on clean datasets that could be used directly for visualizations, data analysis or machine learning tasks.

Given that I enjoyed it more and more to deal with these topics I started studying Applied Computer Science in October 2020 to get more data processing competence.

My general goal is to support decision making with mathematical, statistical analysis.
