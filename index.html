My name is Markus Müller

I have a M.Sc. in Business Psychology and I am currently studying Applied Computer Science (B.Sc.) in the 3nd semester.

During my years at university and the various internships, I always had the most fun when it came to analyzing data and visualizing it for presentations.

I enjoyed working with SPSS (statistical data analysis software) and was responsible for data analysis in any group task. In my bachelor's thesis, I used a two-factor analysis of covariance with two covariates and in my master's thesis, I used moderated multiple regression to answer my research questions.

In February 2020, after completing my Master's in Business Psychology and out of interest in statistical analysis, I began learning Python. I started with the standard libraries for data analysis: NumPy (for mathematical, statistical calculations), Pandas (representation of data in a DataFrame (comparable to Excel)), Matplotlib (visualization of data) and scitkit-learn (standard library for Machine Learning). I learned with Udemy courses, Youtube videos and books.

As I enjoyed it increasingly to deal with these topics and the peculiar situation at that time due to Corona, I started studying Applied Computer Science in October 2020. Currently, I have successfully completed the 2nd semester and am about to start the 3rd semester. Since starting the computer science studies, I have been able to develop my programming skills and general understanding: Object Oriented Programming with Java, relational databases with SQL, or data structures and algorithms. Besides my studies I actively try to improve my skills in my own projects (under Portfolio I have listed a few of my projects):

  • Lightweight GUI to display CSV documents.
  • Collecting tweets from the #66DaysofData challenge and analyzing them.

Goal

My general goal is to support decision making with statistical and mathematical analysis of data to provide insights, using various tools such as Python, SQL, Tableau, SPSS or even MS Office.
