My name is Markus Müller

I have a M.Sc. in Business Psychology and I am currently studying Applied Computer Science (B.Sc.) in the 2nd semester.

During my years at the university and the various internships, I always had the most fun when it came to evaluating data and visualize it for presentations.

Already during my studies I liked to work with SPSS (software for statistical data analysis) and was always responsible for the data analysis in every group task. In my bachelor thesis, I used a two-factor analysis of Covariance with two covariates and in my master thesis, I used a Moderated Multiple Regression to answer my research questions.

After my Master's in Business Psychology, I started learning Python out of the interest in statistical analysis. I started with the standard libraries for data analysis: NumPy (for mathematical, statistical calculations), Pandas (representation of data in a DataFrame (comparable to Excel)) and Matplotlib (visualization of data). I learnd from Udemy-courses, youtube videos and free ebooks. These Udemy courses also included Machine Learning, which introduced the scitkit-learn library (standard library for Machine Learning). To get more serious I succefully completed the nanodegrees for Data Analyst and Data Scientist from Udacity. These courses where more advanced and had a strong focus on data gathering, wrangling and preprocessing, which was somewhat new to me, since I was working on clean datasets that could be used directly for visualizations, data analysis or machine learning tasks.

Given that I enjoyed it more and more to deal with these topics I started studying Applied Computer Science in October 2020. Currently, I have successfully finished the 1st semester and I am about to start with the 2nd semester.

Goal

My general goal is to support decision making with mathematical, statistical analysis and creating visualizations to convey insights, using various tools like Python, Tableau, MS Office or SPSS.
